<Section icon={Target} title="Core Gameplay Loop: The Warclone Cycle">

Survival in **EVE Vanguard early access** requires a deep understanding of the "Warclone Cycle." This is an extraction-adventure FPS where the objective is not just to kill, but to scavenge and survive. Every deployment into a hostile planetary battlefield is a calculated risk. You enter with a basic loadout and must decide whether to pursue high-value contracts or focus on gathering rare resources to upgrade your future capabilities.

The pressure escalates the longer you stay on the surface. While the initial minutes might involve basic skirmishes against Mordu's Legion, the later stages of a match introduce deadlier Nemesis threats and rival Warclones who are just as desperate to extract as you are.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Deploy Planetside">
    Start your run by launching from the Warbarge. You will land in a contested zone, such as the Lost Convoy battlefield, with your selected loadout and a primary objective.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose Your Objective">
    Consult your tactical map immediately. Decide if you will hunt for Resonant Cyphers, raid crash sites for exotic tech, or fulfill specific combat contracts for empire rewards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Raid and Salvage">
    Search wreckage and installations. Use your hacking and salvaging skills to secure advanced technology and relics. Remember that inventory space is limited, so prioritize high-value items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Adapt and Fight">
    Engage PvE enemies like Mordu's Legion or navigate PvP encounters. Use the environment for cover and switch between energy and kinetic damage types depending on your target's shielding.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Extract Before Closure">
    As the mission window nears its end, extraction points will become active but contested. You must reach these zones and defend them until the extraction window opens to secure your loot.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Death Penalty">
In the current Alpha phase, failing to extract means losing everything you gathered during that deployment. Only the items you successfully bring back to the Warbarge can be used for crafting and permanent progression.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Technical Specifications and PC Requirements">

To participate in the **EVE Vanguard early access** playtests, your hardware must be capable of rendering the highly detailed environments of New Eden. The game utilizes advanced lighting and physics to create an immersive sci-fi atmosphere, which places a significant load on the GPU and CPU.

The developers have specified that an SSD is mandatory. Traditional hard drives are too slow to load the large-scale planetary assets, which could lead to stuttering or disconnects during critical extraction moments. Additionally, the game employs Kernel-level Easy Anti-Cheat (EAC) to ensure a fair competitive environment for all Warclones.

| Component | Minimum Requirement | Recommended Specification |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **OS** | Windows 10/11 64-bit | Windows 11 64-bit |
| **CPU** | Intel i5-8400 / Ryzen 5 1600X | Intel i5-11600K / Ryzen 5 5600X |
| **GPU** | RTX 2060 / RX 5700 XT | RTX 3080 / RX 6900 XT |
| **VRAM** | 6 GB | 8 GB+ |
| **RAM** | 16 GB DDR4 | 16 GB DDR4 |
| **Storage** | 50 GB SSD | 50 GB NVMe |
| **DirectX** | Version 12 | Version 12.1 |

<Callout type="info" title="Performance Note">
The current Alpha is optimized to run at approximately 60 FPS on "Low" settings using FSR or DLSS Quality mode on the minimum spec hardware. For the best experience at 1080p or higher, the recommended specs are highly advised.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Shared Universe: The EVE Online Connection">

One of the most unique aspects of **EVE Vanguard early access** is its direct connection to EVE Online. This is not a standalone shooter; it is a ground-based front in a much larger war. Actions taken by Warclones on the surface can trigger events in space, and vice versa.

During Operation Avalon, Warclones can recover "Resonant Cyphers." These items can be traded with empire representatives in EVE Online for valuable SKINs, Skill Points, and rare components. This "One Universe, One War" philosophy ensures that your progress in Vanguard feels meaningful within the broader lore of New Eden.
