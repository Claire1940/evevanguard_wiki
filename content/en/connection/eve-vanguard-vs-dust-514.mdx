While DUST 514 broke ground as the first console-based MMOFPS linked to a PC space game, EVE Vanguard arrives in 2026 as a sophisticated, modern extraction shooter designed to integrate seamlessly with the current EVE Online ecosystem.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics: Arena vs Extraction">

When analyzing **eve vanguard vs dust 514**, the most significant difference lies in the gameplay loop. DUST 514 followed a traditional "match-based" structure where players fought for control points. Once the match ended, the results influenced planetary ownership in EVE Online's Faction Warfare.

EVE Vanguard adopts the **extraction shooter** model. Every deployment is a self-contained mission where you drop into a hostile planetary battlefield, such as the Lost Convoy battlefield in Operation Avalon. You must complete contracts, fight Mordu's Legion or rival Warclones, and reach an extraction point before time runs out.

<Section icon={Zap} title="Connecting to New Eden: The Shared War">

The "One Universe, One War" philosophy remains the core of both games, but the implementation has changed. In the **eve vanguard vs dust 514** comparison, the connection to EVE Online has moved from direct tactical support to strategic world-building.

In DUST 514, a player in space could fire a literal laser beam from their ship to destroy a tank on the ground. In EVE Vanguard (specifically during Operation Avalon in July 2026), the connection is handled through **World Events**. Warclone actions on the ground trigger "AEGIS squalls" and reveal valuable sites in space for EVE Online capsuleers.
