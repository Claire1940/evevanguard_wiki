<Section icon={Map} title="EVE Vanguard vs Frontier: Core Philosophy and Overview">

The EVE Online universe is expanding rapidly in 2026 under Fenris Creations. For players trying to decide between investing their time in **EVE Vanguard** or **EVE Frontier**, understanding their fundamental design philosophies is essential. While both games share the dark, uncompromising DNA of New Eden, they target completely different genres, gameplay loops, and technical architectures.

**EVE Vanguard** is a high-octane, tactical extraction-adventure FPS. Players deploy planetside as immortal Warclones, completing contracts, fighting hostile forces, and extracting valuable resources. On the other hand, **EVE Frontier** (formerly known as Project Awakening) is a hardcore, single-shard space survival simulation where players awaken from stasis to rebuild a shattered region of space.

| **World Structure** | Session-based tactical deployments | Persistent, single-shard open world |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Loop: Warclones vs. Space Survivors">

The moment-to-moment gameplay reveals the starkest contrast between these two titles. EVE Vanguard places you directly in the boots of a boots-on-the-ground soldier, while EVE Frontier keeps you behind the cockpit of a spacecraft navigating hazardous cosmic anomalies.

### EVE Vanguard: The Ground War
In Vanguard, you are an infomorph consciousness projected into a biological combat shell. Every deployment into zones like the *Lost Convoy* is a race against time. You drop in, scavenge advanced technology, engage in brutal PvPvE firefights against rival Warclones and Mordu's Legion, and secure an extraction point before you are overrun.

### EVE Frontier: Cosmic Reconstruction
Frontier strips away the safety of civilized space. You pilot a customizable ship, beginning with a basic vessel "Seed" and expanding it with modular parts. Your survival depends on managing electromagnetic, thermal, and gravitational status effects while fighting off feral AI drones and parasitic "Leeches" that drain your resources.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Technical and System Requirements Comparison">

Because these games run on entirely different engines, their hardware demands vary significantly. EVE Vanguard's Unreal Engine 5 integration requires a modern gaming rig to maintain stable framerates during intense firefights. EVE Frontier runs on the Carbon Engine, which is highly optimized for rendering massive space environments and hundreds of player structures simultaneously.

| Hardware Component | EVE Vanguard Requirements | EVE Frontier Requirements |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minimum OS** | Windows 10/11 (64-bit) | Windows 10/11 or macOS |
| **Minimum CPU** | Intel i5-8400 / AMD Ryzen 5 1600X | Dual Core @ 2.0 GHz |
| **Recommended CPU** | Intel i5-11600K / AMD Ryzen 5 5600X | Intel i7-7700 / AMD Ryzen 7 1700 |
| **Minimum RAM** | 16 GB DDR4 | 4 GB RAM |
| **Recommended RAM** | 16 GB DDR4 | 16 GB RAM |
| **Minimum GPU** | NVIDIA RTX 2060 / AMD RX 5700 XT | NVIDIA GeForce 420 / AMD Radeon 5450 |
| **Recommended GPU** | NVIDIA RTX 3080 / AMD RX 6900 XT | NVIDIA GTX 1060 / AMD Radeon RX 580 |
| **Storage Space** | 50 GB free space (SSD required) | ~20-30 GB free space |

<Callout type="info" title="Performance Note">
EVE Frontier is highly accessible and can run on older hardware or laptops. EVE Vanguard is a heavy next-gen shooter; you will need at least a mid-range modern GPU to hit 60 FPS at 1080p.
</Callout>
