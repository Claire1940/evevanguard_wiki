<Section icon={Terminal} title="Troubleshooting the 'Cannot Look' Bug">

The most prevalent issue with **eve vanguard controller support** is the inability to look around. Users report that while they can move, reload, and shoot, the right analog stick provides zero input for camera rotation. This appears to be a software conflict where the game fails to assign the "Look" axis to the controller hardware.

To resolve these issues, players should follow a specific sequence of configuration steps through the Steam client, as the in-game menus do not yet provide the necessary tools.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Steam Input">
    Right-click EVE Vanguard in your Steam Library, select **Properties**, then **Controller**, and set the override to **Enable Steam Input**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select a Community Layout">
    Enter the **Controller Layout** configurator and search for community-made templates specifically designed to fix the "Look" axis for Vanguard.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Calibrate Deadzones">
    Because the game lacks in-game deadzone settings, use the Steam calibration tool to prevent stick drift, which is common in the current build.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Verify Axis Mapping">
    Ensure the Right Joystick is mapped to **Joystick Mouse** rather than **Joystick Move** to bypass the internal look bug.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip: Virtual Devices">
If you have software like vJoy or other virtual joystick drivers installed, disable them. EVE Vanguard often prioritizes these "ghost" controllers over your actual Xbox or PlayStation hardware.
</Callout>
