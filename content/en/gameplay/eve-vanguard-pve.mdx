**eve vanguard pve** is a sophisticated extraction-adventure experience set within the sprawling universe of New Eden. As an immortal Warclone, your primary objective is to deploy onto hostile planetary surfaces, fulfill high-stakes contracts, and extract with valuable resources before the battlefield becomes untenable. Unlike traditional arena shooters, the PvE component of EVE Vanguard emphasizes risk assessment over pure kill counts. Resources salvaged during a successful run are transferred back to your Warbarge, where they can be used to craft advanced weapon chipsets, upgrade suit systems, and prepare for even more dangerous deployments. This creates a meaningful link between your tactical performance on the ground and your long-term power as a combatant.

<Section icon={Map} title="Contracts and Battlefield Objectives">

The heart of the **eve vanguard pve** experience lies in its contract system. These are not merely side quests but the structural foundation of every deployment. Contracts push you into dangerous territories, such as corporate-controlled installations or crash sites, where the highest concentration of loot and enemies reside. In Operation Avalon, these objectives are often linked to wider New Eden events, meaning your ground-level actions can influence the state of the interstellar war above.

| Mission Type | Primary Objective | Difficulty | Key Rewards |

Salvage raids demand speed and spatial awareness to locate hidden caches, while Data Hacking requires you to defend a static position while your systems bypass corporate encryption. The introduction of the "Lost Convoy" battlefield has added a layer of environmental storytelling, where players must navigate wreckage to find specific mission items while avoiding high-density AI patrols.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Engaging Mordu's Legion and AI Threats">

Combat in **eve vanguard pve** is characterized by its "rebuilt gunplay," which emphasizes weight, recoil management, and tactical positioning. Your primary adversaries are the forces of Mordu's Legion—elite mercenaries with advanced AI that allows them to flank, use cover effectively, and suppress players with heavy fire. Understanding their behavior is essential for surviving high-tier contracts.

In **eve vanguard pve**, shields are highly resistant to kinetic fire but melt under energy beams, while physical armor is best shredded by high-velocity kinetic rounds.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Mastering the Extraction Window">

The most critical phase of **eve vanguard pve** is the extraction. In Operation Avalon, extraction points are not static or guaranteed. As the mission progresses, certain landing zones may become compromised or close entirely, forcing players to move deeper into hostile territory to find a way out. This "risk-based deployment" model means the longer you stay, the harder it is to leave.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor the Extraction Timer">
    Keep a constant eye on the session clock. As the timer winds down, the number of available extraction points decreases.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Landing Zone (LZ)">
    Once you call for extraction, Mordu's Legion forces will likely attempt to intercept. Secure the perimeter before the dropship arrives.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Defend the Extraction Sequence">
    The dropship takes time to land and cycle its doors. Use deployable cover or the environment to survive the final wave of AI attackers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure Your Loot">
    Only items in your inventory at the moment of successful extraction are saved to your persistent Warclone profile.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Failure to extract results in the total loss of all resources and gear collected during that run. While your Warclone is immortal and will respawn on the Warbarge, the physical items you carried are left behind on the planet's surface. This makes the final sprint to the dropship the most intense part of the **eve vanguard pve** experience.

<Callout type="info" title="Tactical Retreat">
If the LZ is too hot, don't be afraid to move to a secondary extraction point. It is better to spend an extra three minutes traveling than to lose thirty minutes of progress in a failed stand.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Weapon Systems and Loadout Management">

Preparation on the Warbarge is just as important as your performance on the ground. The loadout system in **eve vanguard pve** is built around modular firearms that can be tailored for specific roles. Whether you prefer the fast-paced action of an SMG or the precision of a Laser DMR, your choice of chipsets and secondary gear like the Nova Blade will define your playstyle.

| Weapon Role | Best For | Key Feature | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CQC Specialist** | Close Quarters, Raids | SMG, Nova Blade | High mobility, flanking |
| **Marksman** | Long Range, Defense | Laser DMR, Beam Rifle | High precision, shield stripping |
| **Heavy Gunner** | Group Control, Bosses | Shrapnel Cannon | High splash damage, suppressive fire |

Chipsets are the core of weapon customization. These items are found during deployments and allow you to modify fire modes (burst, full-auto) and damage types. For a balanced **eve vanguard pve** build, we recommend a primary weapon with high kinetic damage for finishing off enemies and a secondary energy weapon for dealing with shields.

<Callout type="tip" title="Loadout Optimization">
The Nova Blade is not just for show. In close-quarters combat, a melee strike can often finish an enemy faster than reloading, saving you precious seconds during an intense firefight.
</Callout>
