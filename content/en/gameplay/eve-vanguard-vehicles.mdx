<Section icon={Truck} title="Lost Convoy: Tactical Vehicle Objectives">

The **eve vanguard vehicles** found on the planetary surface are currently centered around the "Lost Convoy" battlefield. In Operation Avalon, this site serves as a graveyard of armored transports and corporate logistics vehicles that have been intercepted or destroyed. These vehicles are not just background assets; they are the primary source of high-tier loot and the focal point for intense PvPvE firefights.

The Lost Convoy represents a shift in how players interact with the environment. Instead of simple scavenging, players must navigate the wreckage of these massive transports, which often contain "Command Blocks" or other critical technology tied to the Nemesis storyline. These vehicle graveyards are protected by elite Mordu's Legion forces, making them the most dangerous zones on the map.

<Callout type="warning" title="High Risk Zone">

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Interact with Battlefield Vehicles">

Interacting with vehicles in EVE Vanguard requires a tactical approach. Since you cannot currently hop into a driver's seat and roam the map, your interaction is focused on salvaging, hacking, and using them for cover. Following a specific protocol when approaching these sites will significantly increase your survival rate during the Operation Avalon playtest.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Scout the Perimeter">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Core">
    Locate the Command Block or primary cargo container within the vehicle. These are usually highlighted during specific contracts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear the Defenders">

  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Salvage and Extract">

  </Step>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Extraction Ships and Deployment Pods">

The lifecycle of a deployment is bookended by two specific **eve vanguard vehicles**: the Deployment Pod and the Extraction Ship. Your journey begins with a high-velocity drop from the Warbarge in a pod that slams into the surface, and it ends with a desperate signal to an extraction craft.

The extraction process is the most critical phase of any mission. Unlike other shooters where extraction might be a simple timer, in EVE Vanguard, the availability of these transport vehicles changes based on the "risk-based" deployment system. As the match progresses, extraction opportunities may disappear, forcing you to push deeper into the map to find a remaining craft.
