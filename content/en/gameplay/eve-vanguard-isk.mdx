<Section icon={Shield} title="Evacuation Survival & Loot Protection">

The extraction phase is the most dangerous part of any deployment. Initiating an evacuation alerts nearby players to your location, turning the extraction zone into a high-stakes combat arena. Knowing when and where to call for extraction is critical to protecting your hard-earned loot.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Perimeter">
    Clear the immediate area of NPC enemies and scan for signs of hostile player activity before calling the extraction beacon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deploy the Beacon">
    Activate the extraction beacon. Be prepared for nearby players to converge on your position once the signal is live.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Establish Defensible Positions">
    Utilize natural cover, high ground, and crossfire angles to defend the beacon until the extraction transport arrives.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Board and Extract">
    Board the transport quickly once it lands. Ensure your squad mates cover the entry points during the final boarding sequence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Choosing the right extraction zone can mean the difference between a successful run and losing all your gear. Different extraction zones offer varying levels of defensive cover and player traffic.

| Zone Profile | Player Density | Defensive Cover | Risk Level |
| **Swamp Edge** | Low | Moderate | Low |
| **Wreckage Site** | High | High | High |
| **Jungle Clearing** | Moderate | Low | Medium |

When playing in a squad, establish clear lines of sight covering the approach paths to the beacon. If you are playing solo, consider waiting nearby in cover until the extraction timer is almost complete before moving to the landing pad.

<Callout type="info" title="Extraction Beacons">
The extraction signal is visible to all players in the sector. Treat every extraction call as an active combat event.
</Callout>
