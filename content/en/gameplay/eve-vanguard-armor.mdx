<Section icon={Shield} title="Introduction to Warclone Armor Systems">

In the brutal landscape of New Eden, your **eve vanguard armor**—professionally known as the Warclone Suit—is the only thing standing between you and permanent termination. Unlike traditional shooters, EVE Vanguard utilizes a persistent gear system where every deployment carries significant risk. Your suit is not just a cosmetic choice; it is a modular platform that dictates your survivability, carrying capacity, and tactical utility on the ground.

**Video Highlights:**
- **Persistent Universe**: EVE Vanguard is directly linked to the 20-year history of the EVE Online MMO.
- **Contraband Mechanics**: Players can find "lost loot" from EVE Online ship battles on the planet's surface.
- **Extraction Stakes**: Failing to extract means losing a portion of your gathered resources and suit progress.
- **Warclone Legacy**: As a Vanguard, you are a ground-based extension of the Capsuleer influence.

<YouTubeEmbed videoId="yfWKUT5n7gs" title="EVE Vanguard Gameplay and Suit Overview" />

The armor system is designed to be iterative. As you participate in **Operation Avalon** (running from July 7 to July 20, 2026), you will notice that the environment itself is your greatest enemy. Whether you are navigating poisoned swamps or hazardous jungles, your suit's integrity determines how long you can stay in the "Dead Zone" before needing to extract.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Early in the alpha, focus on upgrading your **Shield Rechargers** first. Kinetic damage is common from NPC enemies, but environmental hazards will chip away at your health if your shields stay down for too long.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Crafting and Upgrading Your Armor">

Upgrading your **eve vanguard armor** requires a steady supply of resources found within the various points of interest (POIs) in New Eden. The crafting system is tiered, meaning you must first master basic survival before you can equip high-end military-grade plating.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Resource Acquisition">
    Scavenge wreckage of warships and explore poisoned swamps to find raw materials like Polycarbons and Reactive Metals.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Blueprint Research">
    Acquire suit blueprints by completing contracts or finding rare data fragments in Nemesis encounters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Module Fabrication">
    Use the crafting terminal in your home ship to convert raw materials into functional armor modules.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Suit Integration">
    Equip the new modules into your Warclone Suit, ensuring you do not exceed the suit's power capacity.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Material Type | Rarity | Location | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Scrap** | Common | Warship Wreckage | Basic Repairs |
| **Reactive Metals** | Uncommon | Industrial Sites | Kinetic Plating |
| **Toxic Flora** | Rare | Poisoned Swamps | Bio-hazard Resistance |
| **Contraband** | Exotic | EVE Battle Sites | High-tier Augments |

<Callout type="warning" title="Risk of Loss">
Remember that high-tier armor modules are not permanent. If you are defeated and fail to extract, there is a significant chance that your most valuable suit components will be lost or damaged beyond repair.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="The Contraband Connection">

One of the most unique aspects of **eve vanguard armor** progression is the link to the EVE Online MMO. When massive player-run battles occur in the space above the planets, 30% of the destroyed loot is technically "lost." In EVE Vanguard, this lost gear manifests as **Contraband**.

Finding and extracting this contraband allows you to trade with Capsuleers (EVE Online players) through a specialized market. This creates a circular economy where your ground actions are fueled by the destruction happening in the stars.

| Loot Source | Potential Reward | Risk Level | Market Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **NPC Convoys** | Standard Materials | Moderate | Low |
| **Nemesis Encounters** | Advanced Blueprints | High | Moderate |
| **Ship Wreckage** | Contraband Crates | Extreme | High |
| **Hidden Caches** | Rare Tech Modules | Low | Variable |

<Callout type="success" title="Economic Advantage">
Selling contraband back to the original owners or on the open market provides you with specialized currency. This currency is the only way to purchase **Prototype-grade armor** that cannot be crafted through normal means.
</Callout>

</Section>
