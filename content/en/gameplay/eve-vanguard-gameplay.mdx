<Section icon={Target} title="Core EVE Vanguard Gameplay Mechanics">

The foundation of **EVE Vanguard gameplay** is built upon the "extraction-adventure" loop. Unlike traditional arena shooters, every session in New Eden’s ground war is a gamble. You enter the battlefield with a specific loadout, but your primary goal is to return with more than you started with. The game blends intense PvEvP combat with deep sandbox elements, requiring players to balance aggression with tactical retreat.

As a Warclone, you are an immortal soldier whose consciousness is transferred between bodies. This lore-driven mechanic justifies the "roguelike" nature of the game. When you deploy, you are dropped into a persistent war zone where other players and sophisticated AI factions, like Mordu's Legion, are competing for the same limited resources.

<Table>
| Game Phase | Primary Objective | Key Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Deployment** | Tactical insertion into the Lost Convoy or planetary zones | Immediate contact with rival Warclones |
| **Engagement** | Completing contracts (Hacking, Combat, Salvaging) | Resource depletion and ammo scarcity |
| **Looting** | Scavenging crash sites and corporate installations | Inventory weight and movement penalties |
| **Extraction** | Reaching a designated zone before the window closes | Campers and escalating environmental hazards |
</Table>

<Callout type="tip" title="Tactical Awareness">
Always check your surroundings immediately upon landfall. The first 60 seconds of any deployment are the most dangerous, as spawn points can be contested by aggressive squads looking for early kills.
</Callout>

The contracts you choose at the start of a run define your trajectory. Some contracts focus on "Hacking" corporate terminals, which requires a stealthier approach, while "Combat" contracts might task you with wiping out a Mordu's Legion forward base. "Salvaging" is perhaps the most critical for long-term progression, as it provides the raw materials needed to upgrade your gear in the Warbarge between missions.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Systems and Weapon Customization">

Combat in EVE Vanguard is visceral and lethal. The gunplay has been rebuilt for the 2026 Operation Avalon update, emphasizing weight, recoil management, and damage types. Understanding the difference between Kinetic and Energy damage is vital for defeating shielded enemies or armored vehicles.

Players have access to a variety of modular weapons that can be adapted to different combat scenarios. The introduction of Chipsets allows for deep customization, enabling you to switch fire modes or enhance specific weapon stats like reload speed or projectile velocity.

<Section icon={Map} title="Extraction and Looting Strategy">

The most critical decision in **EVE Vanguard gameplay** is when to leave. The "Lost Convoy" battlefield in Operation Avalon introduces a redesigned extraction loop where timing is everything. As the session progresses, the planet "fights back" harder. Mordu's Legion reinforcements become more elite, and the environmental hazards increase in intensity.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Objective">
    Complete your primary contract to unlock the initial extraction data. This ensures you have a baseline reward.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Loot High-Value Zones">
    Target crash sites or the Command Block for rare tech and Resonant Cyphers. This is the highest risk phase.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Monitor Extraction Windows">
    Extraction points are not permanent. Watch the map for "Active" status and move toward the closest zone early.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defend the Zone">
    Once the extraction sequence starts, you must hold the area for a set duration. This often attracts nearby players.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="The Greed Trap">
Staying for "just one more crate" is the leading cause of Warclone death. If your inventory is full of rare salvage, prioritize extraction over further exploration.
</Callout>

Looting is not just about grabbing everything you see. You must manage your inventory space and prioritize items based on their utility. Resonant Cyphers are currently the most sought-after items, as they can be traded for massive rewards in EVE Online. However, these items often emit a signal that can be tracked by other players, turning you into a high-value target.

<Table>
| Loot Category | Utility | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tech Relics** | Weapon chipset crafting | Rare |
| **Resonant Cyphers** | EVE Online reward exchange | Ultra-Rare |
| **Scrap Metal** | Basic armor and ammo repairs | Common |
| **Medical Supplies** | On-field healing and buffs | Uncommon |
</Table>
