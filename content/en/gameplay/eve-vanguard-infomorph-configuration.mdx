<Section icon={Shield} title="Neural Enhancement and Interfacing Skills">

To effectively manage your **eve vanguard infomorph configuration**, you must master the new Neural Enhancement skill tree. This system bridges the gap between the traditional EVE Online character and the Vanguard operative. The core of this system lies in two primary interfacing skillbooks that dictate which "mode" your infomorph can currently inhabit.

| Skill Name | Requirement | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Warclone Interfacing** | Level I to V | Unlocks warclone blanks and movement |
| **Capsuleer Interfacing** | Level I to V | Allows pod piloting and hangar access |
| **Neural Psychology** | Level III | Reduces mental strain during jumps |
| **Infomorph Mode Shift** | Level II | Decreases cooldown between mode swaps |

Training these skills allows for a more flexible playstyle. For example, a capsuleer who wishes to experience planetary combat must inject the Infomorph Warclone Interfacing Skillbook. Level I provides the basic ability to inhabit a warclone blank, while higher levels unlock advanced weaponry and tactical gear.

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Switching between capsuleer and warclone modes often incurs a cooldown. You cannot be active in both modes simultaneously on a single character. Plan your activities to avoid being locked out of a fleet fight or a planetary deployment.
</Callout>

The skill queue for these modes is separate. While you are in "Warclone Mode," your warclone-specific skills will train, whereas "Capsuleer Mode" focuses on your traditional ship-based skills. This dual-track progression ensures that your development as a Vanguard does not stall your progress as a pilot in New Eden's broader theatre.
