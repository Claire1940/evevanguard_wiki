<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Mechanics and Damage Types">

Success in **eve vanguard pvp** requires a deep understanding of the game's modular weapon systems. Weapons in EVE Vanguard are not static; they can be modified with chipsets to alter their performance. A core mechanic involves switching between damage types to efficiently strip enemy defenses. Most Warclones utilize a combination of shields and armor, and using the wrong damage type can significantly increase your Time-to-Kill (TTK).

| Damage Type | Primary Target | Effectiveness | Visual Indicator |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Kinetic** | Armor / Health | High against physical plating | White/Yellow sparks |
| **Energy** | Shields | Rapidly depletes blue bars | Blue/Cyan glow |
| **Hybrid** | Balanced | Moderate against all types | Mixed effects |

When engaging in **eve vanguard pvp**, the standard tactical loop involves using energy-based fire modes or weapons (like the Beam Rifle) to collapse an opponent's shields, then quickly swapping to kinetic fire (like the SMG or Shrapnel Cannon) to shred their remaining armor. Players who fail to manage this "shield-gate" mechanic will find themselves at a severe disadvantage against experienced veterans.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Extraction Tactics and Survival">

The extraction phase is where most **eve vanguard pvp** encounters occur. As the mission timer nears its end, extraction points become hotspots for desperate Warclones. Successfully leaving the planet requires more than just reaching the zone; you must defend it until the transport arrives.

In Operation Avalon, the extraction mechanic has been redesigned to be more contested. Rival players can see when an extraction is triggered, effectively marking your location on the map. This creates a "king of the hill" scenario where you must fend off waves of AI and potentially multiple squads of players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Approach">
    Never run directly into the center of an extraction zone. Approach from high ground or through concealed routes to spot potential campers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Call-In">
    Once the area is clear, trigger the extraction and immediately move to a defensive position. Do not stand directly on the beacon.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Holdout">
    Use grenades and suppressive fire to keep enemies at bay. If a rival squad arrives, prioritize survival over kills—you only need to stay alive until the timer hits zero.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Tactical Retreat">
If the extraction zone becomes too crowded with rival players, it is often better to abandon the site and head for a secondary extraction point if time permits. Losing your loot is worse than losing a few minutes of time.
</Callout>

- **Suit Upgrades**: Permanent improvements to your Warclone's physical attributes, such as stamina and carrying capacity.
