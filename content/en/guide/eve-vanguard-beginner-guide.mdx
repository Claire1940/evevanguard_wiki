<Section icon={Map} title="The Core Gameplay Loop: Deploy, Loot, Extract">

The fundamental structure of the **EVE Vanguard beginner guide** revolves around a high-risk, high-reward cycle. Every deployment is a timed mission where you must balance your objective completion against the ever-increasing danger of the environment and other players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Planetfall and Objective Selection">
    Upon landing in the Lost Convoy battlefield, check your map for active contracts. Objectives range from salvaging exotic technology at crash sites to assaulting corporate-controlled forward bases. Early in your career, prioritize low-risk resource gathering to build up your initial stash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Salvaging and Combat">
    Search wreckage and installations for advanced technology and relics. You will encounter Mordu's Legion—elite AI forces that protect valuable loot. Be prepared for PvE firefights, but stay alert for the sound of other Warclones. In EVE Vanguard, every encounter with another player is a potential betrayal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Gear Adaptation">
    As you gather resources, use your Warbarge between runs to modify your weapons. The modular gun system allows you to switch between Energy and Kinetic damage types. Adapting your damage output based on the enemies you expect to face is crucial for efficiency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Extraction Decision">
    Extraction points are not permanent. As the session progresses, extraction windows will open and close across the map. You must decide whether to push deeper toward the Command Block for legendary loot or head to an early exit to secure what you have already found.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Activity | Risk Level | Primary Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crash Site Raid** | Low/Medium | Basic salvage, weapon parts |
| **Forward Base Assault** | High | Advanced tech, Resonant Cyphers |
| **Command Block Push** | Very High | Rare relics, Nemesis story items |
| **Resource Gathering** | Low | Crafting materials, ammo |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Dying before extraction means losing everything in your inventory. Unlike many other shooters, there is no "participation trophy" in EVE Vanguard. If you don't make it to the ship, you leave with nothing.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapons and Loadout Management">

Success in EVE Vanguard is heavily dependent on your loadout. The game features a modular weapon system where "chipsets" and fire modes can be swapped to suit your playstyle. Operation Avalon has introduced several new weapon types that expand the tactical options available to Warclones.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Close Quarters">
    - **SMG**: High fire rate for tight corridors.
    - **Shrapnel Cannon**: Devastating at point-blank range.
    - **Nova Blade**: Melee finish for silent takedowns.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mid to Long Range">
    - **Laser DMR**: Precision energy weapon for open fields.
    - **Beam Rifle**: Sustained damage for suppressing enemies.
    - **Adaptive Rifle**: Versatile tool for all-purpose combat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Utility & Tech">
    - **Kinetic Chips**: Best against physical armor.
    - **Energy Chips**: Effective against shields.
    - **Modular Scopes**: Essential for identifying threats early.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

When building your loadout, consider the "Damage Type Switching" mechanic. Steam and official documentation highlight the ability to switch configurations mid-run. This is vital when moving from a PvE area (where enemies might be weak to Energy) to a PvP encounter where Kinetic rounds might be more effective against a rival Warclone's suit.

| Weapon Type | Damage Mode | Best Engagement Range |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SMG** | Kinetic/Burst | 5m - 15m |
| **Laser DMR** | Energy/Semi-Auto | 30m - 100m |
| **Shrapnel Cannon** | Kinetic/Spread | 2m - 10m |
| **Beam Rifle** | Energy/Continuous | 20m - 50m |

<Callout type="success" title="Tactical Tip">
Always carry a mix of damage types. Using the wrong damage type against a shielded Mordu's Legion commander can double your time-to-kill, leaving you vulnerable to third-party attacks from other players.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Cpu} title="System Requirements and Performance Optimization">

EVE Vanguard is a visually demanding title built on modern architecture. To ensure a competitive edge, you need to maintain a stable 60 FPS. The game utilizes Easy Anti-Cheat (EAC) at the kernel level to ensure fair play, which is a standard requirement for high-stakes extraction shooters.

| Component | Minimum Requirements | Recommended Specs |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **OS** | Windows 10/11 64-bit | Windows 10/11 Latest |
| **CPU** | Intel i5-8400 / AMD Ryzen 5 1600X | Intel i5-11600K / AMD Ryzen 5 5600X |
| **GPU** | RTX 2060 / Radeon RX 5700 XT | RTX 3080 / Radeon RX 6900 XT |
| **RAM** | 16 GB DDR4 | 16 GB DDR4/DDR5 |
| **Storage** | 50 GB SSD | 50 GB NVMe |
| **DirectX** | Version 12 | Version 12.1 |

Performance can be further optimized by using FSR or DLSS "Quality" modes, which the official site recommends for hitting 60 FPS on minimum-spec hardware at 1080p. Given the 50 GB storage requirement, installing the game on an NVMe drive is highly recommended to reduce loading times between the Warbarge and the planet surface.

<Callout type="info" title="Hardware Note">
EVE Vanguard requires a broadband internet connection. High latency can lead to "desync" during extraction, which may result in a failed mission even if you are standing in the extraction zone.
</Callout>

</Section>
